how video games saved us during a pandemic 

Video games have become an invaluable resource during lockdown, it has let players to temporarily escape from the awful situation that we are in by and allowing them to still have fun with their friends. Online video games serve as positive social interaction at a time when we all must isolate ourselves inside to keep each other safe, this has helped prevent loneliness and more serious mental issues.

It is not just online games that can nurture mental health either, single player games have the power to transport players to other worlds and lose themselves in them. They serve to unwind from a day of work and stress, while keeping their brains active at a time when it is very easy to shut yourself off.

However, it is true that video games can have some negative effects, just as they serve as a way to escape from stress, they can also be an easy distraction from work and exercise. If they are not careful, players can easily become addicted to gaming and their routine and sleep will suffer for it. This can become serious during lockdown as people may not leave their house for weeks, as they have no reason to and they would rather play games, this results in a downward spiral of mental and physical health.

Online games also come with their own problems, when playing competitively it is very easy to get frustrated with the game and fall into a toxic and destructive mindset, which can damage your life and relationships outside of the game, leaving you feeling worse than before you started playing.

Video games have been extremely helpful during this period, but like all things, video games need to be played in moderation, with a positive mindset.
